The Titans

While there is no direct defintion of the word Titans and what it means in Ancient Greek Theogony, the best guess that we have made is based on a Greek root word that losely means, "Over-Reacher". Ouranos gave them this name because they went to far (Reached over/past the boundaries) by taking control of the world. Below is a list of some of the most important Titans.

  • Themis - One of the earliest known titans, she represents the natural orer of things, or law and order. The direct translation to English is "Right".
  • Okeanos - Means Ocean, the water that circled the world. She is extremely fertile. The Greeks believed that the edge of the 'disk of Earth' was made up of Ocean.
  • Pheobe - Light, Shining (She is the god that finally brings light to the time of day)
  • Kronos - He is a shapeshifter.
  • Atlas - Has no real significance yet.
  • Rhea - Like her mother in regards to being an Earth goddess type but she is not as smart as her mother. Very attractive, possibly more so than her mother.
